• Volvox

    Volvox is a genus of freshwater algae found in ponds and ditches, belonging to the Chlorophyta class. They form spherical colonies of up to 50,000 cells. Each cell in the colony has two flagella, which coordinate to propel the sphere through water. Volvox is a model organism for studies on cellular differentiation and biofilm formation.

  • Human Cheek Cells

    Human cheek cells are epithelial cells that line the inside of the mouth. They are flat, thin cells that are easily accessible and often used in basic biology experiments to demonstrate cell structure under a microscope. Cheek cells can reveal nuclei, cytoplasm, and cell membranes, making them excellent for introductory microscopy studies.
